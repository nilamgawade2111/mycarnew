Tyre Maintenance Tips

Proper tyre maintenance extends tyre life, improves safety, and saves money. Follow these expert tips to get the most from your tyres.

1

Regular Pressure Checks

Check your tyre pressure at least once a month and before long trips. Under-inflated tyres wear faster, reduce fuel economy, and can be dangerous. Find the correct pressure on your door jamb sticker or owner's manual.

Pro tip: Check pressure when tyres are cold for accurate readings.
2

Rotation Schedule

Rotate your tyres every 8,000 to 12,000 kilometres to ensure even wear. Front tyres typically wear faster due to steering and braking forces. Regular rotation extends overall tyre life significantly.

Pro tip: Rotate tyres during your regular oil change service.
3

Tread Depth Monitoring

Check tread depth regularly using the built-in wear indicators or a tread depth gauge. Minimum legal depth is 1.5mm, but we recommend replacing tyres at 3mm for optimal safety, especially in wet conditions.

Pro tip: Use the 20-cent coin test - if you can see the platypus's bill, it's time for new tyres.
4

Alignment Checks

Have your wheel alignment checked annually or if you notice uneven wear, pulling, or vibration. Proper alignment prevents premature wear and ensures your vehicle handles safely and predictably.

Pro tip: Get alignment checked after hitting significant potholes or kerbs.
5

Storage Tips

When storing seasonal tyres, keep them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and ozone sources like electric motors. Store them flat or hanging, and consider tyre bags to prevent degradation.

Pro tip: Mark tyres with their position before removal for easy reinstallation.
